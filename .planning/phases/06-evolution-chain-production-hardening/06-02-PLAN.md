# Plan 06-02: Delta Portfolio, Trial Isolation, And Trajectory Evidence

## Objective

Implement the lightweight portfolio and trial mechanics behind Phase 6. A
request may trial one selected delta in isolation, run the full chain, record a
compact trajectory, update computed belief from observed outcomes, and restore
the main path. Durable evidence is bounded by sedimentation instead of storing
every trace forever.

## must_haves

- truths:
  - D-07: durable adoption of a delta into live skills is deferred behind future review, approval, and rollback gates.
  - D-09: trial probability is portfolio-adaptive and accounts for exploration pressure, sample scarcity, production pressure, token budget, and recent failure rate.
  - D-12: every trajectory can enter the short-term buffer, but not every trajectory becomes durable evidence.
  - D-13: trajectory analysis and sedimentation are controlled steps that pause evolution work before the main line resumes.
  - D-14: durable evidence uses a lightweight combined filter for deduplication, information gain, diversity, and local-optimum avoidance.
  - D-16: random trial selection is harness-native and belongs at the SEERS request/scenario boundary, not a Claude Code hook dependency.
  - D-26: posterior update is explicit, testable, lightweight, and based on trial frequency, observed outcomes, token-cost change, and applicability.
  - Delta selection is adaptive but lightweight: sample scarcity, posterior
    belief, token pressure, recent failure rate, and applicability affect trial
    choice.
  - Trial execution applies at most one delta and restores the original skill
    surface on success and failure.
  - Trajectories record request/scenario id, node artifact paths, tool-call
    count, token usage if present, delta id if trialed, and failure category.
  - Sedimentation deduplicates repeated patterns and preserves diversity across
    task, skill, failure, success, and token cases.
  - No durable live-skill adoption occurs.
- forbid:
  - Building a daemon, scheduler service, limiter, or circuit breaker.
  - Mutating `workflow-skills/current/` permanently.
  - Treating schema/tool-contract failures as a privileged online evolution path.
  - Storing raw `user_state`, private reasoning, or full provider messages in durable evidence.

<threat_model>
T-06-04: selection logic over-explores a poor delta or ignores sample scarcity.
Mitigation: deterministic tests cover selection behavior under scarcity, token pressure, and recent failures.

T-06-05: a failed trial leaves the skill surface patched.
Mitigation: tests force exceptions during trial and assert original file content is restored.

T-06-06: evidence storage grows without bound or stores private data.
Mitigation: sedimentation deduplicates by signature and privacy scan rejects private terms before durable write.
</threat_model>
